Wildlife animals Worldwide

Explore some of the most powerful animals in the world, each with unique characteristics and habitat.

The African Elephant is the largest land animal with strong trunks and impressive tusks, found across Sub-Saharan Africa.

African Elephant

Size: Up to 13 ft (4 m)
Weight: Up to 12,000 lbs (5,443 kg)
Location: Sub-Saharan Africa

The Bengal Tiger is known for its striking orange coat with black stripes, and it is a powerful predator found primarily in India and Bangladesh.

Bengal Tiger

Size: Up to 10 ft (3 m)
Weight: Up to 700 lbs (317 kg)
Location: India, Bangladesh

The Grizzly Bear is a large and powerful predator, known for its strength and agility, found in North America.

Grizzly Bear

Size: Up to 8 ft (2.4 m)
Weight: Up to 1,500 lbs (680 kg)
Location: North America

The Saltwater Crocodile is the largest living reptile, known for its powerful bite and adaptability, found in Southeast Asia and Australia.

Saltwater Crocodile

Size: Up to 23 ft (7 m)
Weight: Up to 2,200 lbs (998 kg)
Location: Southeast Asia, Australia

The Lion, often called the "king of the jungle," is known for its majestic mane and social behavior, living in prides in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Lion

Size: Up to 13 ft (4 m)
Weight: Up to 420 lbs (190 kg)
Location: Sub-Saharan Africa

Rhinos are large, thick-skinned herbivores known for their horns, found in Africa and South Asia.

Rhino

Size: Up to 10 ft (3 m)
Weight: Up to 2,300 lbs (1,043 kg)
Location: Africa, South Asia

The Leopard is a solitary and elusive big cat known for its spotted coat and adaptability, found in Africa and parts of Asia.

Leopard

Size: Up to 6.5 ft (2 m)
Weight: Up to 200 lbs (90 kg)
Location: Africa, Asia

The Warthog is a wild pig with distinctive tusks and facial warts, commonly found in Africa.

Warthog

Size: Up to 5 ft (1.5 m)
Weight: Up to 330 lbs (150 kg)
Location: Africa
